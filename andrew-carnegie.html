Andrew Carnegie

The man whose American Dream came true

Andrew Carnegie arrived to Ellis Island penniless and with a hunger for success. The New World promised all who are determined will prosper. Though it is debated how equal opportunity was in this time, Carnegie truly embodied the dream climbing the ladder from railroad worker to the largest steel tycoon in the world.


Timeline:

November 25, 1835: Carnegie was born in Dunfermlime, Scotland.
1848: Carnegie arrives at Ellis Island, aged 13.
1865: Carnegie quits working at the railroad to focus on his investments.
1892: Carnegie Steel Company founded.
July 6, 1892: Homestead Strike, employees rioted over wage cuts. Massive failure for Carnegie.
1901: Carnegie sold his business to focus on philanthropy.
1904:Carnegie-Mellon University founded, now a leading establishment in the Computer Science field.
August 11, 1919: Carnegie dies in Lenox, Massachusetts aged 83.
